Evviva is a cooperative puzzle game for 1-many players.

You play as a team of surgeons, working together to revive a strange patient. You will use square tiles to stitch up their organs, tissues and blood vessels.

As you operate on your strange patient, they will suffer several haemorrhages, and they might even die of a heart attack. But if you manage to stitch up all the body tiles while keeping your patient alive, you all win!

Setup

Your operation always begins with the ü´Ä heart tile.

Place the heart tile face up in the middle of the table and put 3 blood drop tokens on it.

Blood drops mark open wounds, and your patient's heart tile starts with 3 of them:

  1. On the top side of the tile, there's an organ that is open because it is not (yet) fully encompassed by a red membrane.
  2. On the left side, there's a blood vessel that is open because on the left end it does not (yet) connect to an organ.
  3. Similarly, on the right side there's an open blood vessel that doesn't (yet) end in an organ.

Place the heart points tracker tiles face up next to each other on the table, and put the heart token on 5. This is your patient's initial health state. Put the remaining blood drop tokens next to the heart points tracker.

Shuffle the rest of the tiles and stack them face-down to make the draw deck.

Play

The player who was in a real operating theatre most recently takes the first turn. Play then proceeds clockwise.

Here is the full turn breakdown:

1. Top up your hand

Your hand is a set of face-up tiles from which you choose what to play.

If your hand has less than 2 tiles, draw as many tiles from the deck as you need to have 2 face-up tiles.

If you're playing with other people, you will share the same hand.

2. Place 1 tile

Attach 1 tile from your hand to the body, making sure that:

  • You attach at least one side of the new tile to the full side of a tile already in play.
  • The features of each side you attach match the features of the side it joins.

‚ö†Ô∏è In the rare case that none of the tiles in your hand can be legally placed, discard one of them, lose 1 heart point and draw another tile. Repeat this until you can legally place at least one tile from your hand.

2.a Mark any new open wounds

Inspect each open side of the tile you just placed to check if you have opened one or more wounds:

  • If the open side has a blood vessel put a blood token on it.
  • If the open side has an organ that is not already marked as open, put a blood token on it.

2.b Score heart points for any closed wounds

Inspect each closed side of the tile you just placed to check if you have closed one or more wounds.

If the closed side connects to a blood vessel, remove the blood token on the other side.

  • If the blood vessel has no open ends at this point, score 1 heart point for completing it (regardless of its length).

If the closed side has an organ, check if all the organ parts are fully encompassed by a red membrane and there are no gaps within the organ. In that case, you completed the organ:

  • Remove the blood token from the organ.
  • Score 1 heart point for each part of the organ you just completed (for example, a completed organ made of 4 parts will score 4 heart points).

‚ö†Ô∏è Your score can never exceed 18 heart points.

2.c Activate any special organ ability

If the tile you just placed has a special organ, activate it:

Brain
Play smart

Draw the top 5 cards from the draw pile, then put them back in your preferred order (face-up if you like).

Eye
See further

Draw 3 extra tiles.

Lung
Get oxygen

If you completed a blood vessel with the lung, score 1 heart point for each blood vessel part you closed (as opposed to 1 heart point for the whole vessel).

Liver
Cleanse it

Place one card from your hand at the bottom of the draw pile.

2.d Trigger a haemorrhage!

If the tile you just placed has a haemorrhage icon on it, you must trigger a haemorrhage:

  • Count how many blood drop tokens (open wounds) are currently on the body.
  • Lose that number of heart points.

Turn over

Continue taking turns until you have either won or lost the game!

Game over

Lose

You lose the game immediately if any of these three conditions are met at any point in the game:

  1. Bled to death = your heart points drop to zero or below.
  2. Clumsy surgeon(s) = you must add a drop token to the body but you have none left in the supply.
  3. Heart attack = your hand is full of haemorrhage tiles.

Win

If you manage to play all the tiles, then you could win. After you placed the very last tile from your hand (and opened/closed any wounds with it) trigger one last haemorrhage: count how many open wounds are left in the body, and lose that many heart points. If your heart score is above zero, the patient is still alive and you all win the game!

Your next game

Try starting with a number of heart points lower than 5, and/or playing with a number of drop tokens lower than 12 to make your next game harder.

If instead you want an easier operation, start with more heart points and/or more drop tokens!
